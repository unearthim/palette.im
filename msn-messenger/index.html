MSN Messenger

1999 – 2013

"Nudges and emoticons. The butterfly logo fluttered away when Skype took over."

The butterfly that defined teenage communication in the 2000s. "What's your MSN?" was the question that mattered. Custom emoticons, colored text, nudges that shook your screen—MSN Messenger made chat playful. Then Microsoft bought Skype and killed the butterfly.

99
1999 — MSN Messenger 1.0

The Blue Beginning

Microsoft blue from the start. Clean, professional, trustworthy. The butterfly hadn't arrived yet—this was still Windows Messenger, competing directly with AIM and ICQ. The palette was conservative: blues, greys, whites. Microsoft branding through and through.

05
2005 — MSN Messenger 7.0

The Butterfly Era

The golden age. The butterfly logo became iconic. Orange accents appeared for activity and energy. Green status indicators. The interface became playful—gradients, translucency, personality. This was peak MSN: 330 million users, custom emoticons, display pictures, and that screen-shaking nudge feature. The color palette reflected joy.

09
2009 — Windows Live Messenger

The Rebrand

Microsoft rebranded to "Windows Live" and the palette matured. Deeper blues, more sophisticated gradients. The butterfly remained but felt more corporate. Orange toned down. The playfulness was fading as Facebook Chat and smartphones changed the game. Still popular, but the writing was on the wall.

Light Blue #0099FF

13
2013 — The Skype Merger

The Butterfly Dies

Microsoft killed MSN Messenger and forced everyone to Skype. The butterfly palette vanished. Skype's cyan blue replaced the warm MSN blues. The orange was gone. The playfulness was gone. Users mourned. The butterfly that connected a generation was absorbed into a corporate video calling tool. On April 8, 2013, MSN Messenger went dark.
