Friendster

2002 – 2015

"Collapsed under its own success. Technical failures drove users to Facebook. Pivoted to gaming, then died."

The original social network. Before Facebook, before Myspace—Friendster invented the friend graph, the profile page, the testimonial. It had 3 million users in its first 3 months. Then the servers melted. Load times hit 40 seconds. Facebook launched. The rest is history.

02
2002 — Friendster 1.0

The Social Pioneer

Warm, inviting green—the color of friendship. The original Friendster was clean, optimistic, simple. White backgrounds let profiles shine. Blue links were welcoming. This was social networking in its purest form: connecting friends of friends, building community. 3 million users in 3 months. Then the servers crashed.

2005 — The Redesign

Fighting Back

Friendster tried to modernize. Brighter colors appeared—pink accents for personality, yellow for highlights. The green remained but the palette got busier. They were competing with Myspace's customization and Facebook's clean interface. Page load times were still terrible. Users were leaving. The palette felt desperate.

11
2011 — The Gaming Pivot

Social Network No More

Friendster gave up on social networking. It became a gaming platform in Asia. Orange dominated—the color of gaming energy. The green was gone. Purple and yellow exploded across the interface. This was chaos. The original vision was dead. It was now just another casual gaming portal, and not a good one.

15
2015 — The End

Complete Shutdown

On June 14, 2015, Friendster shut down completely. All profiles, photos, testimonials—deleted. The gaming pivot failed. The original green that promised friendship was a distant memory. Grey and black marked the final days. The platform that invented social networking died in obscurity, having sold its patents to Facebook for $40 million.
