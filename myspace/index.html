Myspace

2003 – 2016

"Facebook stole its soul. News Corp bought it for $580M, sold it for $35M. The digital graveyard."

Tom was everyone's first friend. A generation learned HTML by copying code snippets to customize their profiles—glitter text, autoplay music, tiled backgrounds. It was chaotic, beautiful, and yours. Facebook came with its clean blue-and-white uniformity and killed the creative web. Myspace died not because it failed, but because self-expression lost to conformity.

2003-2005 — The Golden Era

The iconic blue navigation bar against the white profile canvas. But the real palette was the millions of custom backgrounds, glitter graphics, and marquee text users added. Myspace's colors were a suggestion—your profile was yours.

2010 — The Desperate Rebrand

Dark Mode Nobody Asked For

After losing to Facebook, Myspace tried to rebrand as a music platform. Black backgrounds, purple accents. It looked cool but felt empty. The customization was gone. The users were gone. This was Myspace pretending to be Spotify.

2013-2016 — The Zombie Years

White Void

Another rebrand. Clean, minimal, modern—everything Myspace wasn't. It looked like every other startup. The blue was muted. The personality was gone. By 2016, it was officially dead. Tom had long since cashed out and was traveling the world taking photos.
