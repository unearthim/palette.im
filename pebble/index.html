Pebble

2012 – 2016

"The smartwatch that Apple crushed. E-paper screens and genuine innovation, gone."

The Kickstarter miracle. Pebble raised $10.3 million in 2012—a record at the time. It had a beautiful e-paper display, week-long battery life, and genuine innovation. Then Apple announced the Apple Watch. Pebble couldn't compete. Fitbit bought the assets for $23 million and shut it down. The servers went dark. The watches became bricks.

12
2012 — The Kickstarter

Orange Hope

Vibrant orange—the color of innovation and energy. The Kickstarter campaign exploded. $10.3 million raised. The branding was bold: orange watch faces, white backgrounds, clean sans-serif type. This was tech optimism embodied. The e-paper display was monochrome, but the brand palette screamed possibility.

13
2013 — Shipping & Growth

The Glory Days

Watches shipped. The app ecosystem grew. The palette expanded: multiple watch colors (black, white, red, orange, grey). The interface stayed minimal—black, white, orange accents. E-paper limitations meant bold, high-contrast design. Battery lasted a week. Users loved it. Then rumors started about Apple's smartwatch project.

2015 — Apple Watch Launches

The Competition Arrives

Apple Watch launched. Pebble tried to compete with Pebble Time—adding color e-paper. The palette became more sophisticated: reds, golds, blacks. But the writing was on the wall. Apple's marketing budget was infinite. Pebble's Kickstarter funding was finite. The orange dimmed. Darker reds appeared. The brand was fighting for survival.

16
2016 — Shutdown

The Lights Go Out

December 7, 2016. Fitbit acquired Pebble's assets for $23 million—a fraction of what the company raised. The servers shut down. The watches became bricks. No more updates. No more app store. The orange was gone. Only black remained. Pebble users mourned. They'd backed innovation and watched Apple kill it through sheer market power. The e-paper dream died.
